⚙ Documentation
Adding Locations
  • Type any city (e.g. Tokyo, Sydney) or country (e.g. Germany, Australia) in the search bar.
  • Select a match from the autocomplete dropdown, or press Enter to pick the first result.
  • Click + Add or press Enter to confirm. The same timezone cannot be added twice.
  • Remove a location by clicking the × button in its label. Your list is saved automatically across sessions.
Reading the Timeline
  • The column headers (00–23) represent UTC hours — a shared universal clock.
  • Each row represents one of your friend's locations.
  • Each cell shows the local hour in that location's timezone for the corresponding UTC hour. Half-hour and 45-minute offsets (India, Nepal, etc.) are handled automatically.
  • The timeline scrolls horizontally. On load it centres around the current time.
Colour Coding
  • Green — 10:00–22:00 local. Prime gaming hours.
  • Amber — 08:00–10:00 or 22:00–00:00 local. Early morning or winding down.
  • Red — 00:00–08:00 local. Your friend is almost certainly asleep.
The NOW Line
  • The gold vertical line marks the current UTC time — it updates every 30 seconds.
  • The current UTC hour column is also highlighted across all rows.
  • Each friend's label shows their current local time, refreshed automatically.
Scheduling a Session
  • Hover any hour cell to see a tooltip listing every friend's local time at that UTC hour.
  • Click any hour cell to open the Schedule Session panel.
  • Choose a date, edit the event title, set a duration, then click Open in Google Calendar.
  • A pre-filled Google Calendar event opens in a new tab, including all friends' local times in the description. Invite your friends from there.
Gear Interactions
  • Every gear in the interface is clickable.
  • Clicking a gear makes it spin at full speed for two seconds, with a gold glow, before settling back to its normal pace.
  • This has no functional effect whatsoever, but it is extremely satisfying.
Data & Privacy
  • All timezone data is computed entirely in your browser using the built-in Intl API.
  • Your location list is stored in localStorage — nothing is sent to any server.
  • This is a fully self-contained HTML file. No internet connection required after loading.
